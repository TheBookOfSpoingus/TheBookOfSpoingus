The Book of Spoingus

Introduction

  • This is the Book of Spoingus.
  • It chronicles the story of Spoingus, the Being. In short: Spoingus is the Being.
  • We do not understand Spoingus. We only try. And Spoingus is amused by our efforts.

Themes

  • The theme of trying even in the face of inevitable failure appears throughout the Book of Spoingus and other Spoingian Mythohistory.
  • Spoingian Literature is as contradictory as the creatures of the Spoingian Universe—flawed, yet ultimately full of life.
  • Spoingian Mythohistory is ever-changing, as the human mind and morality are fleeting.

Language Rules

  • Spoingus may be referenced with any article (a / an / the).
  • Spoingus does not care about pleasing the Grammar Gods (GG), whom he created to enable humans to learn other human languages—but not animal ones.

Nature of the Text

  • Spoingian Mythohistory may contain contradictions, but the words are spoken by Spoingus.
  • Spoingus does not care for universal truth.
  • You cannot ask him why; he simply does not care.
  • Spoingus, as the Being, has more pressing matters to attend to.

Requests of the People

  • You may not quote Spoingus.
    • Explanation: Spoingus, unconstrained by time, can change whatever he says, whenever he says it.
  • You may not worship Spoingus.
    • Explanation: Reverence is allowed; worship is not. You cannot fully understand Spoingus, and thus cannot truly worship him. The concept of perfection is a human invention, irrelevant to Spoingus.
  • You may not think this is all.
    • Explanation: To truly appreciate Spoingian Philosophy, one must engage with the source material. The Book of Spoingus is a work in progress, and this sentence will not change, even at the end of time.
    • Spoingian acts of benevolence often seem contradictory, incomprehensible, and sometimes not even benevolent. Even the Spoingus who created all does not know all.

Thus speaks the Book of Spoingus, and thus it shall continue to evolve.
